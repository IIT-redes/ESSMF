Curtailment and compensation

When may access be restricted, how much, and with what compensation or protection?

Understand the dimensionComment on this dimension

Clear definition

What this dimension means

Curtailment and compensation define the trigger, priority, notice, frequency, duration, volume, cap, verification, and financial treatment of a restriction on import or export.

Curtailment is the main risk transferred to the connected user. It must be bounded and measurable; otherwise the connection may be technically available but commercially unusable.

Design choices

Choices that must be made explicitly

A reviewer should be able to identify which option is selected, why it is selected, who decides it, and how it is implemented.

Trigger

Measured congestion, voltage, outage, maintenance, contingency, forecast condition, or emergency.

Priority

Pro rata, first connected, technical effectiveness, contractual class, or market-based selection.

Limit

Annual hours, energy volume, event count, duration, or financial exposure.

Compensation

No compensation, reduced connection charge, tariff discount, event payment, or excess-curtailment payment.

Practical example

How the dimension appears in practice

A generator is curtailed during planned maintenance and unplanned congestion. The agreement should distinguish the two, specify notice, establish annual caps, and define what happens if the DSO exceeds the contracted envelope.

Review principle

The mechanism is credible only when the selected design can be explained in plain language and linked to a verifiable network, regulatory, commercial, or customer outcome.

Stakeholder implications

The same design choice creates different responsibilities and risks

DSO

Apply restrictions consistently and only when technically justified.

Regulator

Set minimum transparency, audit, and compensation safeguards.

Developer or aggregator

Quantify revenue-at-risk and manage portfolio exposure.

Customer or FSP

Verify events and access a practical remedy.

Questions for reviewers

What remains open?

  • Which events count as contractual curtailment?
  • How is priority among users determined?
  • What cap makes the risk financeable?
  • When is compensation required?
  • Who verifies whether an event was justified?

Evidence expected

What should support the decision?

  • Curtailment duration curves
  • Event logs and network-state records
  • Revenue-at-risk modelling
  • Comparison of compensation options
  • Independent audit methodology
