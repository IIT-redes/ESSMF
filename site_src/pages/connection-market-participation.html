Market participation and mechanism interaction

Can the connected resource participate in markets without double obligation or conflicting control?

Understand the dimensionComment on this dimension

Clear definition

What this dimension means

Market participation defines whether capacity subject to a flexible connection may be aggregated or offered in local flexibility, balancing, wholesale, or other markets, and how DSO access limits interact with those commitments.

A resource can create value in several markets, but part of its flexibility may already be committed through the connection agreement. Clear boundaries are needed to protect network security while avoiding unnecessary exclusion.

Design choices

Choices that must be made explicitly

A reviewer should be able to identify which option is selected, why it is selected, who decides it, and how it is implemented.

Eligibility

Full participation, participation within the operating envelope, or restricted services.

Availability rule

How connection obligations affect declared market availability.

Control priority

DSO access limit, market activation, aggregator dispatch, and emergency control.

Double commitment

How the same capacity is prevented from satisfying two incompatible obligations.

Practical example

How the dimension appears in practice

A battery with a flexible import connection offers upward balancing and local congestion relief. Its market bids must remain feasible within the DSO operating envelope, and the baseline must not reward compliance that is already mandatory.

Review principle

The mechanism is credible only when the selected design can be explained in plain language and linked to a verifiable network, regulatory, commercial, or customer outcome.

Stakeholder implications

The same design choice creates different responsibilities and risks

DSO

Maintain security while enabling efficient value stacking.

Regulator

Prevent discrimination and unnecessary barriers to market participation.

Developer or aggregator

Know which capacity is commercially available and which is reserved.

Customer or FSP

Receive clear value sharing and avoid conflicting obligations.

Questions for reviewers

What remains open?

  • Which services remain feasible under the access envelope?
  • How is capacity reserved for mandatory compliance identified?
  • What priority applies during conflicting activation?
  • How are baselines and double payment handled?
  • What data must be exchanged with market platforms?

Evidence expected

What should support the decision?

  • Feasibility and co-optimization studies
  • Control-priority scenarios
  • Baseline and double-counting analysis
  • Market-liquidity impact
  • Data and interoperability assessment
